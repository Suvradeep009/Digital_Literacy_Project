\# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) or online payment fraud involves
cybercriminals deceiving individuals into authorizing unauthorized
financial transfers from their bank accounts using digital payment
platforms.

\*\*The Scenario\*\* Anurag, a college student, posted an ad online to
sell his old smartphone. Shortly after, a \"buyer\" contacted him,
agreeing to the asking price without any negotiation. To finalize the
purchase, the buyer claimed they needed to send a small test payment to
verify the account.

\*\*Step-by-Step Execution\*\* 1. \*\*Initial Contact:\*\* The scammer
reaches out via a digital marketplace, establishing false trust by
appearing eager to buy. 2. \*\*The Hook:\*\* Instead of sending money,
the scammer uses the UPI app\'s \"Request Money\" feature to send a
payment request of ₹15,000 to Anurag\'s phone. 3. \*\*The Deception:\*\*
The scammer urgently calls, claiming the money has been dispatched and
instructing the seller to enter their UPI PIN to \"receive\" the funds.
4. \*\*The Theft:\*\* Confused by the app interface and pressured by the
scammer\'s urgency, Anurag enters his PIN. Rather than receiving funds,
₹15,000 is instantly deducted from his own bank account.

\*\*Target Demographic\*\* Scammers frequently target individuals
selling items on second-hand online marketplaces, the elderly, and users
who are less familiar with the specific mechanics of digital payment
interfaces.

\*\*Consequences\*\* The immediate consequence is a sudden, direct
financial loss, which is notoriously difficult to reverse due to the
instantaneous nature of peer-to-peer digital transactions. Victims also
frequently experience emotional distress, a lingering loss of trust in
digital banking systems, and compromised contact information.
